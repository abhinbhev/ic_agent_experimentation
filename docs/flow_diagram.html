Domain knowledge

Planner Consultant & Decision Consultant are domain-agnostic — directional only, no KPIs/dimensions.
Planner & Decision Engine are domain-grounded — given DOMAIN GUIDELINES to translate that direction into concrete KPI/dimension terms.

Worked example

Query: "Why did revenue decline in East China during Q1?"  ·  Domain: example (datasets Sales, Revenue; dimensions Brand, Region)

Round 1 — initial plan continue
Similar Plan

Filters the corpus to patterns that touch Sales/Revenue, then ranks by BM25 + embedding similarity to the query.

Planner Consultant

Drafts hypotheses and directional probe goals -- it knows the question is about Revenue declining in East China, but (being domain-agnostic) doesn't know exact KPI names, units, or what dimensions exist to drill into.

objective: "Identify the main driver(s) of the East China revenue decline in Q1."

hypotheses: [
  { id: "H1", description: "Overall sales activity fell in East China", status: "open" },
  { id: "H2", description: "The typical price or mix of what's sold shifted", status: "open" },
  { id: "H3", description: "One part of the portfolio drove most of the decline", status: "open" }
]

probe_candidates: [
  { id: "P1", goal: "How has overall sales activity in East China changed vs. a year ago?",
    expected_value: "high", reason: "Establishes the size and shape of the decline (H1)." },
  { id: "P2", goal: "Has the typical price or product mix sold in East China shifted?",
    expected_value: "high", reason: "Tests a price/mix shift directly (H2)." },
  { id: "P3", goal: "Is the decline concentrated in one part of the portfolio, or spread broadly?",
    expected_value: "medium", reason: "Tests whether the decline is broad-based or concentrated (H3)." }
]

success_criteria: "Q1 decline is explained in terms of volume vs. price/mix, with the
  largest-contributing portfolio segment identified."
open_questions: []
Planner

Uses DOMAIN GUIDELINES (the example domain's metrics Volume/Revenue and dimensions Brand/Region) to rewrite each directional goal into a concrete question, then routes it to a retrieval usecase. Finally orders by expected_value and caps at max_probes_per_round.

assignments: [
  { probe_candidate_id: "P1",
    question: "What were East China Volume (hL) and Revenue (CNY) in Q1 vs. prior-year Q1?",
    usecase: "category", reason: "Volume/Revenue trend by Region is a category-level query." },
  { probe_candidate_id: "P2",
    question: "How did Revenue per hectoliter (CNY/hL) in East China change in Q1 vs. prior-year Q1?",
    usecase: "category", reason: "Revenue per hL is derived from the same Sales/Revenue dataset." },
  { probe_candidate_id: "P3",
    question: "Which Brand in East China had the largest year-over-year Revenue decline in Q1?",
    usecase: "category", reason: "Brand-level Revenue breakdown within a Region." }
]
→ tool_calls: [P1, P2, P3]  (3 of 3 within budget)

Final report — Synthesizer

Given the full 2-round ledger, the latest Decision Consultant output, and stop_reason = "incremental_value_below_threshold", the Synthesizer writes the markdown report:

## Summary
East China Revenue fell 18% YoY in Q1 (1.56B → 1.28B CNY), driven by a 12% Volume
decline and a 7% drop in Revenue per hectoliter (price/mix shift). Corona accounted
for a disproportionate share of the decline due to a distribution coverage drop.

## Key Findings
| Metric                  | Prior-year Q1 | Q1 (current) | Change |
|-------------------------|--------------:|-------------:|-------:|
| Volume (hL)             | 1.84M         | 1.62M         | -12%   |
| Revenue (CNY)           | 1.56B         | 1.28B         | -18%   |
| Revenue per hL (CNY)    | 850           | 790           | -7%    |
| Corona Revenue (YoY)    | —             | —             | -25%   |

## Evidence
- P1: East China Volume/Revenue trend (relevance 0.95)
- P2: Revenue per hectoliter trend (relevance 0.90)
- P3: Corona Revenue decline vs. portfolio (relevance 0.85)
- P4: Corona distribution coverage 78% → 65% after distributor transition (relevance 0.92)
- P5: No Corona-specific pricing changes found (relevance 0.70)

## Recommendations
- Prioritize restoring Corona's distribution coverage in East China with the new
  distributor (target: back toward 78%).
- Investigate the broader price/mix shift (channel mix vs. promotions) — currently
  only partially explained.

## Confidence
0.7 — the volume and price/mix decomposition and the Corona distribution driver are
well-supported (confidence 0.78 from the Decision Consultant), but the broader
price/mix gap remains partial.

## Remaining Unknowns
- Whether the price/mix shift is driven by promotions, channel mix, or list-price
  changes remains only partially addressed.
