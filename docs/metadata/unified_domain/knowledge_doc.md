# Unified Marketing Analytics Domain

## Overview

This unified domain spans two complementary studies under Strategy & Insights (S&I) within AB InBev's One Way Marketing framework. Together they provide a 360-degree view of brand performance — how consumers **perceive** a brand and how they actually **consume** it.

- **Brand Guidance** (Brand Perception) — survey-based brand health and perception data
- **Category** (Consumer Consumption / POS) — survey-based consumption behavior data

These are distinct but correlated lenses. Strong brand equity may or may not translate to consumption growth; investigating both together reveals whether perception leads behavior or vice versa.

---

## Sub-Domain: Brand Guidance (Brand Perception)

### What it covers
- Survey-based brand health and perception metrics designed by Kantar
- Brand equity measurement: how strong a brand is in consumers' minds relative to competitors
- Emotional and functional connections consumers have with brands
- Awareness, consideration, and trial metrics across the consumer funnel
- Brand imagery and associations (what consumers think of when they think of a brand)
- In-market facilitators and barriers (trade visibility, availability, price perception)

### Scope and granularity
- **Brands:** 600+ brands with hierarchy (brand, brand family)
- **Markets:** Top 32 countries
- **Time periods:** Monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, full-year, rolling windows (3M, 6M, 12M), YTD
- **Demographics:** Age, gender, income, region cohorts
- **Aggregation levels:** Brand, brand family, country, global, zone (NAZ, SAZ, MAZ, APAC, AFR, EUR)
- **Filters:** ABI vs competitor, price tier, portfolio classification, life cycle

### What questions it can answer
- How is a brand perceived relative to competitors in a market?
- What drives a brand's equity position — emotional connections, differentiation, or mental availability?
- How has brand awareness, consideration, or trial changed over time?
- What imagery associations does a brand have and how do they differ across demographics?
- Are in-market factors (visibility, availability, price) helping or hurting the brand?
- How does brand perception compare across countries, zones, or demographic groups?

### What it cannot answer
- Actual consumption volumes, frequency, or serving sizes
- Market share or sales data
- Forecasting or projections
- Share of voice, media metrics, or creative scores
- Statistical significance testing
- Weighted distribution data

## Sub-Domain: Category (Consumer Consumption / POS)

### What it covers
- Survey-based consumption data measuring who is drinking what, how often, and how much
- Purpose: grow the beer category by understanding consumer behavior above legal drinking age
- Three core measures: Participation (who drinks), Occasions (how often), Servings (how much per occasion)
- Coverage across beer and beyond-beer categories including RTD, cider, spirits, wine, non-alcoholic beverages

### Scope and granularity
- **Brands:** Extensive brand list with sub-brands and manufacturers
- **Categories:** Beer, RTD/Cider, Spirits, Wine, Non-Alcoholic, and many sub-categories
- **Markets:** 29 countries
- **Time periods:** Monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, full-year, rolling windows (3M, 6M, 9M, 12M), YTD
- **Demographics:** Age, gender, income cohorts
- **Aggregation levels:** Brand, category, sub-category, manufacturer, price tier, style; by country, zone, market maturity (M1/M2/M3)
- **Filters:** Price tier (core, core+, premium, super premium, value), macrocategory

### What questions it can answer
- What percentage of consumers are participating in a category or brand?
- How frequently are consumers engaging with a brand/category (occasions per week)?
- What is the average serving size per occasion?
- How does consumption differ across demographic groups (age, gender, income)?
- How do consumption patterns vary by geography (country, zone, market maturity)?
- What is the consumption trend over time for a brand, category, or price tier?
- How do manufacturers compare on consumption metrics?

### What it cannot answer
- Brand perception, equity, or health metrics
- Awareness, consideration, imagery, or emotional connections
- Brand associations or in-market facilitators
- Forecasting or projections
- Actual sales revenue or transaction data

## Cross-Domain Relationships

### Shared dimensions
Both domains share common dimensions enabling cross-domain analysis:
- **Brand:** Many brands appear in both studies
- **Country/Market:** Overlapping country coverage
- **Time period:** Same periodization (quarterly, half-yearly, full-year, rolling)
- **Demographics:** Age, gender, income cohorts available in both

### Unique dimensions per domain
- **Brand Guidance only:** Imagery statements, in-market facilitators, brand clarity, brand family aggregation, global brand aggregation
- **Category only:** Price tier, manufacturer, category/sub-category, style, macrocategory, market maturity levels, zone-level aggregation for consumption

### Analytical synergies
- A brand with high equity (Brand Guidance) but low consumption participation (Category) suggests a perception-to-behavior gap
- Rising awareness (Brand Guidance) combined with rising occasions (Category) indicates healthy brand growth
- Declining equity despite stable consumption may signal future consumption risk
- Demographic patterns in perception can be compared against demographic consumption patterns

---

## What the Unified Domain Cannot Answer

- External KPIs: Share of Voice (SOV), Weighted Distribution (WD), media principles
- Actual sales, revenue, or transaction data (both studies are survey-based)
- Forecasting or forward-looking projections
- Statistical significance testing
- Creative scores or brand linkage
- Superiority analysis
- MDS contributions to Power (Brand Structure Analysis)
